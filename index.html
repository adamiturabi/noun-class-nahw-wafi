بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم

الحمد لله والصلاة والسلام على نبينا محمد. أما بعد:

Introduction

Can any noun be feminized with tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h? Can any noun be used as a naɛt? Will a naɛt always agree in number or gender with the manɛūt?

These are some questions that we will attempt to answer. We start with some quotations from books that touch upon these issues. We then present a classification of nouns primarily pieced together from different sections of النحو الوافي لعباس حسن. We follow this with a classification of the naɛt and its conditions. In these sections we will only present the information as we find it in the primary sources. Any comments from ourselves are restricted to within square brackets [] or footnotes.

Next, we look at case studies. We will use the classifications and conditions to determine the behavior of nouns we are studying.

We use primarily Arabic grammatical terminology by transcribing terms and make little attempt to translate them.

The romanization scheme used in this article is ʾbtt͡h jḥk͡h dd͡h rz ss͡h ṣḍ ṭḍ͡h ɛg͡h fq klmn hwy aui āūī aw ay. The tāʾ marbūṭaḧ is transcribed as ḧ and ẗ, the latter when in construct state. Transcribing of technical terms is generally in pausal form.

Some relevant quotations

Classification of nouns

Basic categories

Nouns are classified2 as follows:

  1. jāmid: That which is not taken from another.
    1. ism d͡hāt: Concrete. Examples: شجرة، قلم، أسد، حجر.
    2. ism maɛnā: Abstract. Examples: فَهْم، نبوغ، ذكاء، سماحة.
  2. mus͡htaqq: That which is taken from another, which is its origin. Sometimes the mus͡htaqq is referred to as the waṣf or the ṣifaḧ but by this they don’t mean [the syntactical category]: the naɛt. The original mus͡htaqqāt are seven:
    1. ism al-fāɛil:
    2. ism al-mafɛūl:
    3. al-ṣifaḧ al-mus͡habbahaḧ:
    4. afɛal al-tafḍīl:
    5. ism al-zamān:
    6. ism al-makān:
    7. ism al-ʾālaḧ:

There are also some jāmid nouns which sometimes are admitted to the mus͡htaqq category. These are known as al-ʾasmāʾ al-jāmidaḧ al-mulḥaqaḧ bil-mus͡htaqq or al-ʾasmāʾ al-mus͡htaqqaḧ taʾwīlan. Among these are:

  1. ism al-ʾis͡hāraḧ:
  2. ism al-jāmid al-mansūb:
  3. ism al-jāmid al-muṣag͡hg͡har:
  4. Most of the mawṣūlāt:

These are only sometimes admitted to the mus͡htaqq category. They are only admitted as such in places where only a mus͡htaqq can be used, for example as naɛt, because the naɛt basically has to be mus͡htaqq.

The maṣdar

The maṣdar can be either al-maṣdar al-ṣarīḥ or al-maṣdar al-muʾawwal.

al-maṣdar al-ṣarīḥ is considered the origin of the ten mus͡htaqqāt (the seven mentioned above, and al-fiɛl al-maḍī, al-fiɛl al-muḍāriɛ, and al-fiɛl al-ʾamr). As such, it is from al-ʾasmāʾ al-jāmidaḧ.

al-maṣdar al-ṣarīḥ is of three kinds3:

  1. al-maṣdar al-aṣliyy. It does not start with a supplemental mīm nor does it end with iyyaḧ. Examples: عِلْم، فَهْم، تقدُّم، استضاءة، إبانة، بلاء، نضال، فَضْل، صَلاح. It also includes the maṣdar of al-marraḧ wal-hayʾaḧ.
  2. al-maṣdar al-mīmiyy. It starts with a supplemental mīm and it does not end with iyyaḧ.The correct opinion is that it is not from the mus͡htaqqāt, rather it is jāmid. Examples are مَطْلَب، مَضْيَعة، مَجْلَبة، مَعْدَل. It must needs be singular.
  3. al-maṣdar al-ṣināɛiyy. It is qiyāsiyy, formed from any word (jāmid or mus͡htaqq, noun or non-noun) to which is appended iyyaḧ. This appendage gives it a new meaning like إنسانية “humanity” from إنسان “human”. It is a jāmid mulḥaq bil-mus͡htaqq.

When the word maṣdar is used by itself, by it is intended al-maṣdar al-ṣarīḥ al-ʾaṣliyy.

Patterns of al-maṣdar al-aṣliyy4

al-maṣdar al-aṣliyy of form I verbs can be of the qiyāsiyy patterns: faɛl, fiɛālaḧ, faɛal, fuɛlaḧ, fuɛūl, fuɛūlaḧ, fiɛāl, faɛalān, fuɛāl, faɛīl, faɛālaḧ. There are numerous other samāɛiyy ones like fuɛl, faɛāl, faɛalaḧ.

[al-maṣdar al-aṣliyy of forms II and higher are found easily in grammar references.]

The maṣdar and the ism al-maṣdar.

The maṣdar5 (i.e., al-maṣdar al-ṣarīḥ al-ʾaṣliyy) is a noun that indicates –by and large– only an event. And it contains all the original and supplemental letters which comprise al-fiɛl al-maḍī which is derived from it (excepting standard substitutions to form the regular forms (like سَلَّمَ تَسْليمًا: taslīm has a tāʾ to substitute for one of the lām), substitutions for weak letters (like عِدة for verb وَعَدَ), and deletions due to frequency of usage). It may also contain additional letters, excepting those which form al-maṣdar al-mīmiyy and al-maṣdar al-ṣināɛiyy.

The definition includes two aspects: meaning and form. The aspect of meaning denotes only an event, no time, place, d͡hāt, proper noun, gender, or number (excepting that which is indicated by al-marraḧ wal-hayʾaḧ). The aspect of form defines what letters a maṣdar must comprise.

The ism al-maṣdar6 (which is limited to al-samāɛ) is equivalent to the maṣdar from the aspect of meaning but it differs in the aspect of form. It may not have all the letters of its al-fiɛl al-maḍī. Examples are عَطاء for إعطاء.

The difference between the two in meaning is that the maṣdar directly signifies the verb event, while the ism al-maṣdar directly signifies the maṣdar, thus indirectly signifying the verb event.

Dualization and pluralization of the maṣdar7

The maṣdar is not permitted to be dualized or pluralized as long as what is intended by it is just the meaning [of the verb], i.e. as long as it is mubham. So we cannot say صفحت عن المخطئ صفْحين, nor وعدتُك وعودًا. Except if the maṣdar ends with tāʾ marbūṭaḧ in which it may be dualized and pluralized, e.g. التلاوتان والتلاوات.

If however it is al-maṣdar al-mubayyin li l-nawɛ, when its anwāɛ are different, or al-maṣdar al-mubayyin li l-ɛadad, then it is permitted to dualize and pluralize it if it satisfies the conditions of one of the three kinds of plurals jamɛ mud͡hakkar, jamɛ muʾannat͡h sālim, jamɛ taksīr.

Example for al-maṣdar al-mubayyin li l-nawɛ: سلكت مع الناس سُلُوكَيِ العاقل: الشدةَ حينًا والملاينةَ حينًا آخر. سرت سِيَرَ الخلفاء الراشدين meaning سلكت مع الناس نوعين من السلوك العاقل: الشدة حينًا والملاينةَ حينًا آخر. وسرت معهم آنواعًا من السِيَر. What is intended here is not stating the number of times of السلوك, that it was twice, nor stating the numer of times of السيْر, that it was many times. Rather, what is intended is stating the different kinds.

Example for the al-maṣdar al-mubayyin li l-ɛadad: خطوت في الحديقة عشر خطوات، ودُرتُ في جوانبها أربع دوْرات

Conditions of the jamɛ muʾannat͡h sālim

This plural may be applied with qiyās on six categories of nouns8:

  1. All nouns that end in a tāʾ zāʾidaḧ. This is absolute, whether it is

    • an ɛalam, like فاطمة,
    • or not an ɛalam, like زراعة، تجارة.
    • Feminine in form and meaning, like حليمة، رقية from female names,
    • or feminine in form only, like عطية، حمزة، معاوية from male names.
    • The tāʾ is from feminization as in the precding examples,
    • or for substituting an original [weak] letter, like عِدة، ثُبة, which are pluralized as عِدات، ثُبات,
    • or even for emphasis, like علّامة، علّامات.

    There are some exceptions like امرأة، أَمَة، شاة، شَفَة، قُلّة، أُمّة، مِلّة.

  2. All nouns that end in alif al-taʾnīt͡h al-maqṣūraḧ ʾaw al-mamdūdaḧ. This applies for ɛalam or non-ɛalam, male or female [names], Examples are سُعدى for a female ɛalam, فُضْلى for a feminine ṣifaḧ, دُنيا when it is a male ɛalam, زَهراء for a female ɛalam, حسناء for a female ṣifaḧ, and زكرياء for a male ɛalam. Excepted from this, according to the non-Kufans, are two forms فَعْلى the feminine of فَعْلان like سَكْرى the feminine of سكران, and فَعْلاء the feminine of أفْعَل like خضراء وسوداء both of which are ṣifaḧ for feminine nouns, and not ɛalam.

  3. All ɛalam for female living beings which don’t have an ɛalāmaẗ taʾnīt͡h, like زينب، نوال، إحسان.

  4. muṣag͡hg͡har for masculine nouns which are not ɛāqil, like نُهيرات the taṣg͡hīr of نهر, and جُبَيْلات the taṣg͡hīr of جبل, and مُعَيْدنات the taṣg͡hīr of مَعْدن.

  5. waṣf for non-ɛāqil masculine nouns, like هذه بساتين جميلات, (where for the singular we would have بستان جميل) and زرتها أيامًا معدودات.9

  6. Every k͡humāsiyy for which a jamɛ taksīr has not been heard from the Arabs, like سُرادِقات، قَيصومات، حَمّامات، كتّانات، اصطبلات، قِطْميرات for سُرادِق، قَيصوم، حَمّام، كتّان، اصطبل، قِطْمير.

Nouns which don’t fall under these six categories are restricted to samāɛ like شَمَالات for شَمَال, “the north wind”.

Feminization of nouns10

The tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h is introduced by qiyās on the mus͡htaqqāt to distinguish between the masculine and feminine. Examples are عابِد وعابدة، عرّاف وعَرّافة، فَرِح وفَرِحة، مأمون ومأمونة. Even in the mus͡htaqqāt, the tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h is introduced on most nouns but not all. Some of the mus͡htaqqāt do not admit it at all, according to the opinion of the majoity of the grammarians, while some admit it only sometimes.11

There are other ways of feminizing nouns, like alif al-taʾnīt͡h al-maqṣūraḧ12 and alif al-taʾnīt͡h al-mamdūdaḧ13, both of which are samāɛan. [These are always extraneous to the original radicals.]14.

The tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h is not introduced by qiyās on ʾasmāʾ al-ʾajnās al-jāmidaḧ, but it is sometimes found by samāɛ [for nouns denoting male and female living beings]. Examples are رَجُل ورَجُلة، أَسَد وأسدة، فَتًى ،فتاة، غُلام وغلامة، امرؤ وامرأة، إنسان وإنسانة.

Most nouns denoting male and female living beings, however, have different words for them. Examples are ثَور وبقرة، حِمار وأتان، تَيس وعنز. Sometimes the same word is used for living beings of both gender [although it may be grammatically of one gender]. Examples are حمامة وفرس.15

Classification of the naɛt

Basic categories

The naɛt is classified16 with respect to its meaning into mufrad, jumlaḧ and s͡hibh jumlaḧ.

Categories of qiyāsiyy words which are correct as naɛt mufrad are:

  1. al-ʾasmāʾ al-mus͡htaqqaḧ al-ɛāmilaḧ
  2. Or what is in [accordance with] their meaning.

By al-ɛāmilaḧ is meant

  1. ism al-fāɛil:
  2. ṣiyag͡h al-mubālag͡haḧ:
  3. al-ṣifaḧ al-mus͡habbahaḧ:
  4. ism al-mafɛūl:
  5. afɛal al-tafḍīl:

Those [al-ʾasmāʾ al-mus͡htaqqaḧ] which are not al-ɛāmilaḧ, and which do not occur as naɛt are:

  1. ism al-zamān:
  2. ism al-makān:
  3. ism al-ʾālaḧ:

And what is meant by “what is in [accordance with] their meaning” is all al-ʾasmāʾ al-jāmidaḧ that are similar to al-ʾasmāʾ al-mus͡htaqqaḧ in what they signify in meaning. These are called al-ʾasmāʾ al-mus͡htaqqaḧ taʾwīlan. The most common of these are:

  1. ʾasmāʾ al-ʾis͡hāraḧ g͡hayr al-makāniyyaḧ, like هذا. These are only al-naɛt to definite nouns. Example: استمعت إلى الناصح هذا.

    The ʾasmāʾ al-ʾis͡hāraḧ al-makāniyyaḧ like هُنا، ثَمّ are ḍ͡hurūf makān. They don’t occur by themselves as naɛt. But they may be attached to a maḥd͡hūf which will be the naɛt. Example: أسرع العِطاش إلى ماء هنا, i.e., موجود هنا.

  2. ذو، المضافة meaning “the possessor of so-and-so”. “It is a naɛt to indefinite nouns”. (But some references say it may be a naɛt to definite nouns as well.) Example: أنِست بصحبة عالم ذي خلق كريم.

  3. al-mawṣūlāt al-ismiyyaḧ which begin with a hamzaẗ waṣl. Examples: الذي، التي، اللائي. As opposed to the mawṣūlaḧ أي.

    There is some dispute regarding مَنْ and ما being naɛt. The correct opinion is that it is permissible for them to be naɛt.

    Examples: الضعيف الذي يحترس من عدوه، أقرب إلى السلامة من القوي الذي ينخدع، أو يستهين. The meaning is الضعيف المحترس من عدوه، أقرب إلى السلامة من القوي المنخدع، …. Thus its meaning is that of the mus͡htaqq.

  4. al-ism al-jāmid which signifies al-nasab in purpose. The most common forms are those which end in iyy; or those on the measure faɛɛāl, other measures which signify al-intisāb in purpose. Examples: ألْمحُ في وجه الرجل العربيّ كثيرًا من أمارات الصراحة، والشجاعة، والكفاح، اشتهر الرجل اليوناني بالنشاط ،الهجرة إلى حيث يتسع الرزق أمامه، وفي بلادنا جماعة منهم تمارس الحِرَف والصناعات المختلفة. فتجد بينهم التاجر والبقال واللبان والحداد … و …17

    These kinds of nouns can correctly be naɛt for both definite and indefinite nouns.

  5. al-muṣag͡hg͡har: because it includes a description in its meaning. In this respect it is similar to the nasab. And therefore they are admitted to al-mus͡htaqq.

  6. al-ism al-jāmid al-manɛūt bi l-mus͡htaqq. Example اقتديت برجلٍ رجلٍ شريفٍ. This kind of naɛt is also called al-naɛt al-muwaṭṭiʾ.

  7. al-maṣdar: with the conditions that it:

    1. be indefinite,
    2. be al-maṣdar al-ṣarīḥ,
    3. not be al-maṣdar al-mīmiyy,
    4. not be used as a ṭalab (likeقيامًا للضيف meaning قم للضيف),
    5. be the maṣdar of a form I verb, and
    6. retain its original form with respect to its singularity, masculinity (excepting maṣādir which have an original tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h like رحمة، شفقة). If, however, the use of the maṣdar as a naɛt becomes common then is permisible to dualize or pluralize it qiyāsiyyan. An example is the poet’s verse:
      وبايعتُ ليلى في الخلاءِ ولم يكن *** شهودٌ على ليلى، عدولٌ مَقانعُ 18

    Example: رأيت في المحكمة قاضيًا عدْلا وشهودا صدْقًا ونظامًا رِضًا وجموعًا زَوْرًا بين النتقاضين meaning قاضيًا عادلا وشهودا صادقين ونظامًا مرضيًّا وجموعًا زائرة بين النتقاضين

    The maṣdar can be a taqdīr muḍāf maḥd͡hūf which is the naɛt. That is, originally it is قاضيًا صاحبَ عدل…

    The grammarians differed with respect to the maṣdar occurring as a naɛt, whether it is qiyāsiyy or restricted to al-samāɛ. Most of them restricted it to al-samāɛ, although they acknowledged that it does appear frequently in the corpus, and that it is ʾablag͡h than the mus͡htaqq. This acknowledgement that it appears frequently contradicts their restricting it to al-samāɛ. The correct opinion should then be to take it qiyāsiyyan with its conditions.

    Note: the scholars of al-balāg͡haḧ have proclaimed that al-naɛt bi l-maṣdar is from the category of al-mubālag͡haḧ, or from majāz al-ḥad͡hf, or al-majāz al-mursal, and that the t͡hulāt͡hiyyaḧ is qiyāsiyy. How can the scholars contradict each other, where the the scholars of al-balāg͡haḧ say that al-naɛt bi l-maṣdar is ʾablag͡h than the mus͡htaqq at the same time that some of the grammarians say that al-naɛt bi l-maṣdar, despite its frequency in the corpus, is not qiyāsiyy? How can they say this when the Quran, the most eloquent of speech, contains it multiple times? This contradiction can only be resolved by saying that al-naɛt bi l-maṣdar is qiyāsiyy with the aforementioned conditions. ibn jiniyy in his book al-muḥtasab (vol. ii, p. 46) says, “The usage of al-maṣdar as a naɛt directly, without taqdīr bis͡hayʾ maḥd͡hūf, is ʾablag͡h and ʾalṭaf than the naɛt which is not a maṣdar.” He supports this claim with multiple evidences, and presents many quotations.

    With regards to al-naɛt bi l-maṣdar, ibn mālik says: ونَعتُوا بمَصْدَرِ كَثِيرًا *** فالْتَزَمُوا الإِفْرَاد والتَّذْكِيرَا.

    That is, the Arabs used al-naɛt bi l-maṣdar frequently and did not modify the maṣdar from its form retaining singularity and gender. Thus these two aspects will always be retained even if the manɛūt is not singular and not masculine. For example هذا أمر رِضًا، هذان أمران رِضًا، هذه أمور رِضًا، هذه حالة رِضًا، هاتان حالتان رضا، أولئك حالات رضا.

  8. ism al-maṣdar if it is of a t͡hulāt͡hiyy measure. Examples فِطْر ism al-maṣdar of the verb أفطر meaning مُفْطِر أو صاحب إفطار. You can say هذا رجلٌ فًطْر، ورجلان فِطْر، ورجال فِطْرٌ.

  9. al-ɛadad: like قرأت كتبًا سبعةً، وكتبت صحفًا خمسةً.

  10. Some other words that are jāmid muʾawwal bi l-mus͡htaqq, connoting perfection or deficiency, like the word كُلّ, e.g. عرفت العالِمَ كُلَّ العالمِ

  11. The jāmid that signifies al-ṣifaḧ al-mus͡habbahaḧ together with its acceptability as al-taʾwīl bi l-mus͡htaqq. Examples فلانٌ رجلٌ فَراشةُ الحِلمِ، فِرعونُ العذابِ، غِربالُ الإهابِ. The words فَراشة، فِرعون، غِربال are analyzed as naɛt bi l-mus͡htaqq, because they mean أحمق، قاسٍ، حقير respectively.

    These19 are qiyāsiyy but it is better to employ them as such as little as possible. They may have a supplemental geminated yāʾ li l-nasab that brings them closer to the mus͡htaqqāt. Example تناولنا شرابًا عسلًا طعْمُه or تناولنا شرابًا عسليًّا طعْمُه.

Some additional information with respect to the naɛt

Definite maṣdar as naɛt20

Regarding the condition that the maṣdar be indefinite if it occur as naɛt: It is sometimes found in the corpus as definite: either with al or as muḍāf li maɛrifaḧ. An example with al is the word الحقّ in the poet’s verse:

إن أخاك الحقَّ من يسعى معكْ *** ومن يضرّ نفسه لينفعكْ

Examples of muḍāf li maɛrifaḧ are مررت برجل حسبِك من رجل أو شَرْعِك أو كافيك أو همِّك أو نحوِك. These ought to be definite by ʾiḍāfaḧ but they don’t become definite because they are in the meaning of a mus͡htaqq and that they are a naɛt to an indefinite.21

Another such example is the Quranic aayah هذا عارضٌ مُمْطِرُنا.The word عارضٌ is described by مُمْطِرُنا which is muḍāf ʾilā ḍamīr therefore it does not become definite.22

Examples of naɛt not falling under the preceding classification.23

Some examples of naɛt of are found in the corpus which don’t fall under the preceding classification. For example:

  1. A maṣdar of a non-form I verb, e.g. الحازم لا يعالج الأمر عِلاجًا ارتجالا.
  2. A naɛt signifying quantity, e.g. اشتريت من الفاكهة الخمسَ الأقَقَ.
  3. A naɛt signifying material of a manufactured object, e.g. لبست الثوبَ الحريرَ.
  4. A naɛt signifying al-ʾaɛyān of whom taʾwīl is possible, e.g. حصدتَ الحقل القمح meaning المزروع قمحًا.

It is best that qiyās not be done on these examples.

ism al-ʾis͡hāraḧ as naɛt or manɛūt24

There are some nouns that are correct as naɛt when they satisfy the conditions of naɛt; and other times as manɛūt when they satisfy the conditions of manɛūt. Like ism al-ʾis͡hāraḧ. For example, احتفيت بالمصلح هذا or بهذا المصلح.

Also, ism al-ʾis͡hāraḧ is definite so it can only be a naɛt for a definite noun. And when it is a manɛūt, its naɛt must have the al of definiteness. (And it is better that it be mus͡htaqq. If however, it is jāmid, then it is better that it is considered a badal or ɛaṭf bayān rather than a naɛt.) It is also required that it match its manɛūt in number and gender, and without separating the naɛt. It should also not be separated from its manɛūt, and that it match it in case.

al-ɛalam and al-ʾajnās as naɛt25

al-ɛalam may not be naɛt, nor al-ʾajnās which signify their original meaning like رجل ونمر وفيل. However, if they [al-ɛalam and al-ʾajnās] leave their original signification, and a well-known description is intended by them, then it is permissible for them to be naɛt. For example حاتم signifies الكرم, and الرجل signifies الكامل, and النمر signifies الغادر. In this purpose, they may be interpreted as mus͡htaqq.

Case studies

In this section we will consider how to use the above classifications and rules for specific examples. Regarding the usage of nouns as naɛt, we will only consider whether they may be “apparent naɛt”. This is because some grammarians, for example, will not allow a definite maṣdar to be a naɛt. And when they find apparent examples of this in the corpus, they will analyze it using tools like maḥd͡hūf and taqdīr. They are justified in this analysis because they are building a cohesive minimal framework for grammar. As for us, we will only consider the suitability of a noun as an “apparent naɛt” without worrying about its technical grammatical function.

Can masculine ʾasmāʾ jāmidaḧ be used for females

There are some masculine ʾasmāʾ jāmidaḧ like شَخْص “person” and عُضْو “member”, which can be applied to female persons. Since they are ʾasmāʾ jāmidaḧ, they can not be feminized with tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h except samāɛan. Therefore, they will be used in their original masculine form but other nouns around them should be feminized whereever applicable. Examples:

هي عضوٌ داىمةٌ للجنة
She is a permanent member of the committee.

We find this usage discussed in detail in the article عضو أم عضوة، ومسائل أخر لفيصل المنصور.

We also find the following in الأصول في النحو لابن السّراج26:

من ذلك قول الشاعر:
فكانَ مِجَنّي دونَ مَنْ كنتُ أتقّي *** ثَلاثُ شُخوصٍ كاعبانِ ومُعْصِرُ

فإنما أنّث الشخوص لقصده النساءَ فحمله على المعنى، ثُمّ أبانَ عن إرادته وكشفَ عن معناه بقوله: كاعبانِ ومُعْصِرُ

Usage of al-maṣādir as designative nouns and as naɛt

The definite maṣdar as a naɛt

In the aformentioned conditions maṣdar may only be a naɛt if it is indefinite. However, we find, for example the word ثقة meaning “trustworthy”, in the books of the sciences of ḥadīt͡h, that it also appears as a definite naɛt. This is a technical usage and arguably it is not from “kalām al-ɛarab”, nevertheless it is found. Example27:

6474 م- قال شُعْبة وذَكر لي رجلٌ ثقةٌ عن سفيان أنه كان يقول: إنما قال: “من كان متَحريَها فلْيَتَحرها في السَّبْع البَواقي”، قال شُعْبة: فلا أدري قال ذا أو ذا؟، شُعْبة شَك. [قال عبد الله بن أحمد]: قال أبي: الرجلُ الثقةُ: يحيى بن سعيد القَطَّان.

We have also found one grammar reference that explicitly calls for the maṣdar matching the manɛūt when it is used as a naɛt28:

ويشترط في المصدر النعت أن يكون فعله ثلاثيًا كالمصدر ثقة عدل وألا يكون مصدرًا ميميًا وفي هذه الحالة يلتزم الإفراد والتذكير ولا يطابق المنعوت إلا في الإعراب والتنكير فتقول:

هذا رجلٌ ثقةٌ، هذا الرجلُ الثقة، هؤلاء رجال ثقة

The maṣdar mīmiyy as a naɛt

According to the aforementioned conditions, a maṣdar mīmiyy may not be naɛt. However, we are able to find such cases, even in grammar books (albeit used technically, not as examples from the corpus).

The word مَعْرِفة is used to mean “a definite noun” and it often appears as a tābiɛ. Example:29:

قال الشارح: وأمّا انتصابه محلاًّ فإذا كان المنادَى مفردًا معرفةً؛

Note that معرفة here is unlikely to be مُعَرَّفة (which is synonymous but feminine) because of the masculine fāɛil and mafɛūl bih manɛūt.

Pluralization of the maṣdar as naɛt

Pluralization of the maṣdar as naɛt, too is prescriptively disallowed. However we find this used as well, for example in the title of the book تاريخ ومعرفة الرجال الثقات للعجلي.^[Note as well the separation of the muḍāf from the muḍāf ʾilayh which is sometimes disliked in grammar books but seems to be allowed by ibn mālik:

ويُحْذَفُ الثَّانِيْ فََيَبْقَى الأَوَّلُ *** كَحَالِهِ إِذَا بِهِ يَتَّصِلُّ
بِشَرْطِ عَطْفٍ وإضَافَةٍ إِلَى *** مِثْلِ الَّذِي لَهُ أَضَفْتَ الأَوَّلا

In our opinion, the usage of the singular is preferred. The plural is only palatable if the word ends with tāʾ zāʾidaḧ pluralized with jamɛ muʾannat͡h sālim. Or if a broken plural exists, as عُدول for عَدْل in the verse quoted above.

Feminization and pluralization of descriptive jāmid nouns when used as designative nouns

These jawāmid may be maṣadir like ثقة which we have already considered as a naɛt. When it is used as a designative noun, it can be dualized and pluralized, but its form with regards to its tāʾ zāʾidaḧ is not changed. Examples:

  • رواه الثقةُ
  • رواه الثقتان
  • رواه الثقاتُ

There are other jāmid nouns for which we know no broken plurals nor do they generally satisfy the criteria for jamɛ muʾannat͡h sālim, but which are nevertheless descriptive. Examples are

  • The maṣdar حَلال, but which can also be used qualitatively, meaning “a lawful thing”.30
  • The word فُضُول, which was originally a plural of فَضْل, but which now means “an extra unused item (or items)”

These nouns may be used to designate items (masculine or feminine), and also as naɛt. How are these nouns feminized, dualized, and pluralized?

As for their feminization, then this again is not permitted due their being jāmid.

And as for dualizing them, we find an example in لسان العرب quoting أبو زيد31:

قال أَبو زيد: أَقمتُ بالبصرة صَوْمَينِ أي رَمضانينِ.

The word صَوْم is a maṣdar which here is used to designate the fasting month ramaḍān.

Similarly we extrapolate that we may dualize حلال to حلالان.

As for pluralizing it, we have found it taking jamɛ muʾannat͡h sālim despite not meeting its conditions. However, we have only found this in a later book تفسير الجلالين.32

“يَأَيُّهَا الرُّسُل كُلُوا مِنْ الطَّيِّبَات” الْحَلَالَاتِ

As for the word فضول, since it was originally a plural, we theorize that it should not be pluralized further, except as a jamɛ al-jamɛ.

Rules for foreign borrowings

The Arabic language has borrowed nouns like:

  • أستاذ، دكتور which refer to titles or professions,
  • باشا، خاتون which refer to titles but for specific genders,
  • كمبيوتر، فِلْم which refers to an object, and
  • طازَج، ساذَج which refer to qualities.

The question is how these nouns should be classified, and then if and how they should be feminized, dualized and pluralized.

The correct opinion seems to be that nouns are borrowed into the class in which are nouns most similar to the borrowed noun.33

Thus باشا، خاتون، كمبيوتر، فلم are borrowed into the jawāmid. For the words that refer to living beings, the grammatical gender corresponds to the gender of the living beings. The gender of words referring to non-living items is unclear to us and we defer on discussing it. Either way, these words may not feminized with tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h. They may have jamɛ taksīr if the Arabs have formulated them like خواتين، أفلام for خاتون، فلم, or may take jamɛ muʾannat͡h sālim if not, e.g. باشاوات. Dualization is regular.

And nouns like أستاذ، دكتور، طازج، ساذج are borrowed into the mus͡htaqqāt, where they may be feminized with tāʾ al-taʾnīt͡h. They may take jamɛ taksīr like أساتذة or jamɛ sālim, even jamɛ mud͡hakkar sālim when referring to male human beings. Furthermore, the feminine singular may be used when a naɛt for non-ɛāqil objects, e.g. أَكَلت الأطعمة الطازَجة.

Mus͡htaqqāt that are lexicalized as designative nouns

There are nouns which are originally mus͡htaqqāt but that have become designative nouns. For example:

  • نَصِيحَة “an advice”
  • مُعَلِّم “a male teacher”
  • مُعَلِّمة “a female teacher”
  • كَبِيرَة “a major sin”
  • حَسَنَة “a good deed”
  • جَرِيح “a wounded person”
  • رَهينة “a pledge”

When used with this designative meaning, then their tāʾ zāʾidaḧ, if any, is not modified. Also, the plural is used for non-rational plural subjects/manɛūt. For example:

  • امرأة جريح
  • هذه الأفعال حسنات وتلك الأفعال كبائر “These acts are good deeds and those acts are major sins.” But هذه الأفعال حسنة وتلك الأفعال كبيرة “These acts are good and and those acts are big.”
  • كلّ غلام رهينة بعقيقته34. (Note that رَهين may be used as an epithet (with descriptive/participal meaning) and feminized with tāʾ zāʾidaḧ if needed.)
  • كَلامٌ نصيحةٌ. هذا الكلامُ نصيحةٌ
